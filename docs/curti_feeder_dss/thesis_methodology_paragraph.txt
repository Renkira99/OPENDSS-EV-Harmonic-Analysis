Methodology note (Curti feeder parameter estimation and correction):
The raw Curti feeder survey contained non-physical parameter uniformity in which identical line impedance values were repeated across trunk and lateral sections, and transformer loss modeling was represented using a generic `%Rs` formulation without rating-specific no-load loss data. To obtain a physically consistent feeder model for EV impact studies, line parameters were restructured using hardcoded IS 7098 11 kV 3-core AL XLPE classes (240 sq mm trunk, 95 sq mm medium tap, 35 sq mm small tap), including resistance, reactance, shunt capacitance, and thermal ratings (`normamps`). Transformer losses were recalculated per rating class (100, 160, 200, 400, 500, 1000, and 1250 kVA) from 50% and 100% load-loss watt data by solving the standard two-equation core/copper decomposition and converting to OpenDSS `%LoadLoss` and `%NoLoadLoss`; `Xhl` was then computed from class-wise `%Z` consistency using `Xhl = sqrt(%Z^2 - (%LoadLoss)^2)`. Source stiffness was represented with `MVAsc3=400` at Colony_SS while omitting `MVAsc1` to avoid introducing unverified zero-sequence assumptions. The updated model was validated through snapshot and fault-study runs, with exported before/after metrics confirming convergence, acceptable LV voltage floor, no line overloads, and source short-circuit current alignment with the specified 400 MVA stiffness.
